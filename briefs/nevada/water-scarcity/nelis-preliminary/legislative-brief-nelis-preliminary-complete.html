What This Brief Is About

This preliminary Legislative Brief is a NELIS-only package for internal Forum review. [S4] Synthesis below describes purpose cues and drafting language visible in bill titles. It is not a deliberation guide and not the final OpenStates-enriched Version 0. State Of Play

Agency context materials describe Southern Nevada supply dependence on the Colorado River system and an ongoing state water-planning function. [S1][S2] Across four sessions, NELIS search shows a steady cadence of water-tagged filings (36, 33, 36, then 42 in 2025). [S4]

Title language repeatedly returns to general water-statute revision, groundwater instruments, rights administration, conservation, Colorado River institutions, and DWR capacity. [S4]

How This Works In Nevada (Institutions Named In Sources)

Collected materials place water administration with the Division of Water Resources / State Engineer under Nevada water statutes (including NRS Chapters 533 and 534 as linked in this package). [S1][S10][S11] Southern Nevada supply framing appears in SNWA public materials. [S2] Bill titles additionally name the Colorado River Commission of Nevada and, in SJR3 (2023), the U.S. Bureau of Reclamation. [S4]

What Is Currently Being Introduced (From NELIS Titles)

Conservation and general water statute work. Twenty-eight titles use stock “revises provisions … water” language; four titles expressly address water conservation. [S4]

Groundwater management. Seven titles name basins, boards, assessments, management plans, or groundwater conservation across 2019–2025. [S4]

Colorado River / CRC interface. CRC service and membership measures plus a 2023 Reclamation-focused joint resolution. [S4]

Rights and agency capacity. Diversion/dedication/temporary-change titles, plus multiple DWR appropriations. [S4]
