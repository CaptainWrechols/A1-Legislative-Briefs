## WHAT THIS BRIEF IS ABOUT

This preliminary Legislative Brief shows what a completed briefing package looks like using **Nevada Legislature Information System (NELIS) bill-discovery data only**. It synthesizes the **stated purpose and drafting language** visible in NELIS bill titles for water-related measures filed in the 2019, 2021, 2023, and 2025 regular sessions. It is not a deliberation guide and not the final Version 0 brief. A separate clean Version 0 will be built when enriched action, vote, sponsor, and enactment data are ready. ## STATE OF PLAY

Nevada’s water pressures sit at the intersection of Colorado River reliability, groundwater basin management, conservation rules, and growth-related demand. [S1][S2] Southern Nevada’s supply picture is tied to Colorado River conditions and related institutions; rural and northern questions often appear through groundwater, rights, and basin tools in bill titles. [S2][S4]

Across four sessions, NELIS search finds a steady cadence of water-tagged filings (roughly three dozen per session, 42 in 2025). Generic statute-revision titles are common, while groundwater, rights, conservation, and Colorado River institutions recur as named themes. [S4]

**INSUFFICIENT DATA** for which bills received committee votes, floor votes, crossed over, received second-chamber action, or were signed into law. Those milestones are outside the NELIS discovery fields used for this preliminary package. [S4]

Agency/LCB context documents frame why scarcity appears on the calendar (including data-center background), but they do not identify which of the 147 bills passed. [S3]

## HOW THIS WORKS IN NEVADA (INSTITUTIONS NAMED IN SOURCES)

Collected agency materials place water administration with the State Engineer / Division of Water Resources under state law, including water-rights and groundwater tools. [S1] Southern Nevada supply context includes SNWA description of Colorado River dependence. [S2] Bill titles also name the Colorado River Commission of Nevada and, in at least one resolution, the U.S. Bureau of Reclamation. [S4] A local land-use approval does not by itself create new water; water availability remains a distinct legal and physical question. [S1][S2]

---

## WHAT IS CURRENTLY BEING INTRODUCED (FROM NELIS TITLES)

**Conservation and general water statute work.** Repeated “revises provisions … water” and “water conservation” titles indicate incremental statutory drafting. [S4]

**Groundwater management.** Titles on basins, boards, assessments, management plans, and groundwater conservation recur from 2019 through 2025. [S4]

**Colorado River / CRC interface.** CRC service and membership bills plus a 2023 Reclamation-focused joint resolution. [S4]

**Rights and agency capacity.** Diversion/dedication/appropriation-change titles, plus DWR appropriations for equipment and systems. [S4]
