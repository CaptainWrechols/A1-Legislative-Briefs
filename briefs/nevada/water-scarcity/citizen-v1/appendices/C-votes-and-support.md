# Appendix C — Floor Votes and Support Levels

This appendix shows every recorded *final passage* floor vote for bills in the set — the up-or-down vote a full chamber (Assembly or Senate) takes on whether a bill passes. There are 104 such votes across the four sessions. Committee-stage votes are not tabled here because most committee meetings in the underlying record have no recorded roll call. Party affiliations behind these tallies were matched from the Legislature's legislator directory (with a Ballotpedia fallback); the counts themselves come from the official roll calls. A second, shorter table lists bills that won a majority-Yes floor vote and still never became law.

## High Yes, not enacted

These ten measures won at least one majority-Yes final-passage floor vote but did not become law. Three are joint resolutions, which pass both chambers and are enrolled but never become statutes. A majority vote in one chamber does not prove statewide popularity or that the idea would pass again.

| Year | Bill | Plain topic | Theme | Winning vote(s) | Where it stopped |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2019 | AB30 | Would have let the State Engineer require monitoring-and-mitigation plans, with public hearings, before deciding water permit applications. | Permits | Assembly 31-9 (78%) | Passed first chamber; stopped in second chamber |
| 2021 | AJR2 | Resolution recognizing that healthy forests, rangelands and soils are tied to the amount and quality of water. | Conservation | Assembly 34-8 (81%); Senate 21-0 (100%) | Resolution passed both chambers (enrolled) |
| 2021 | AJR3 | Resolution urging various actions to protect and conserve land and water. | Conservation | Assembly 26-16 (62%); Senate 12-9 (57%) | Resolution passed both chambers (enrolled) |
| 2023 | AB263 | Would have addressed transmission of Legionnaires' disease through building water systems in certain buildings. | Clean water | Assembly 28-14 (67%) | Passed first chamber; stopped in second chamber |
| 2023 | AB387 | Would have required, not just encouraged, the State Engineer to use best available science, and recognized connections between groundwater and surface water. | Disputes & oversight | Assembly 26-14 (65%) | Passed first chamber; stopped in second chamber |
| 2023 | SB180 | Would have changed how groundwater boards are established and dissolved. | Groundwater & wells | Assembly 41-0 (100%); Senate 21-0 (100%) | Passed both chambers; never enacted |
| 2023 | SJR3 | Resolution urging the U.S. Bureau of Reclamation to consider actions and alternatives to protect and manage the Colorado River. | Colorado River | Assembly 42-0 (100%); Senate 21-0 (100%) | Resolution passed both chambers (enrolled) |
| 2025 | SB143 | Would have had a legislative interim committee review water conservation and ordered a state study of artificial turf. | Conservation | Senate 21-0 (100%) | Passed first chamber; stopped in second chamber |
| 2025 | SB31 | Would have revised deadlines and procedures for adjudicating old 'vested' water-right claims. | Disputes & oversight | Senate 15-6 (71%) | Passed first chamber; stopped in second chamber |
| 2025 | SB324 | Would have banned sales of small disposable plastic water bottles in communities abutting the Lake Tahoe watershed. | Clean water | Senate 16-4 (80%) | Passed first chamber; stopped in second chamber |
