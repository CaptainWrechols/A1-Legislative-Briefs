# How to Read These Appendices

These appendices hold the detailed record behind the citizen brief on growth, water scarcity, and long-term supply in Nevada. They cover 88 water-related bills and resolutions from the 2019, 2021, 2023 and 2025 regular sessions of the Nevada Legislature. Everything here describes what happened; nothing here recommends what anyone should do.

## The appendices

| File | What it contains |
|---|---|
| A — Bills overview | One row for each of the 88 bills: topic, theme, result, where it stopped, sponsors. |
| B — Theme scorecards | For each of nine themes: how that kind of idea fared, with examples and a history basket. |
| C — Votes and support | All 104 final-passage floor votes, plus bills that won majority votes and still never became law. |
| D — Sponsors and people | Every sponsor of every bill, frequent primary sponsors, and cross-party bills. |
| E — Bill path details | How far each bill traveled: first committee, first chamber, second chamber, final outcome. |
| F — Data limits | What this data can and cannot tell you. Read this before drawing conclusions. |
| G — Text changes | For the 48 measures that finished, whether the final text was amended along the way. |

## Reading a bill id

Bills appear as a year plus a number, for example **2019 AB163**. "AB" means Assembly Bill, "SB" means Senate Bill, and "AJR"/"SJR" mean Assembly or Senate Joint Resolution. Some tables also show a compact machine key such as `80:AB163` — the number before the colon is the legislative session (80th = 2019, 81st = 2021, 82nd = 2023, 83rd = 2025).

## A few terms, once

| Term | Plain meaning |
|---|---|
| First (origin) committee | The committee a bill is sent to in the chamber where it starts. Most failures in this set stopped here. |
| Final passage | The up-or-down floor vote by a full chamber on whether the bill passes. |
| Second chamber | After passing its starting chamber, a bill must also pass the other one. |
| Enrolled | The final, official version of a measure after both chambers pass it. |
| Joint resolution | A statement or proposed constitutional change; it can pass both chambers but does not become a statute. |
| Chapter | A bill that became law is assigned a chapter number in the session laws. |

## Where the data comes from

All records come from the Nevada Legislature's public NELIS system, collected and processed locally; party labels on roll calls were matched from the Legislature's legislator directory with a Ballotpedia fallback. The set was assembled through water-keyword searches and is not a proven complete universe of water-relevant legislation. Appendix F lists all known limits.
